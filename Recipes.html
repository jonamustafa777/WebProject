French Cuisine

French cuisine has a long and rich history, dating back to the Middle Ages, and is renowned worldwide for its elegance and attention to detail. Influenced by royal courts, regional ingredients, and culinary traditions, French cooking emphasizes fresh, high-quality ingredients and precise techniques. Some of the most iconic dishes include Coq au Vin, Ratatouille, Bouillabaisse, and Crème Brûlée. French cuisine has inspired chefs around the world and continues to set the standard for fine dining, balancing flavors, textures, and presentation in every meal.

Salad Niçoise

Salad Niçoise is a French salad that originated in the Nice region of France in the 19th century. It typically consists of mixed greens, eggs, Niçoise olives, lots of fresh veggies, and anchovies and/or tuna.

  • Produce: New potatoes, green beans, onion, Niçoise olives, mixed greens, plum tomatoes, parsley
  • Dressing: Store-bought lemon vinaigrette
  • Eggs: Hard-cooked eggs
  • Jarred fish: Canned tuna and anchovy filets
  • Capers: 1 tablespoon
Coq au Vin

Coq au vin is a French chicken stew in which chicken thighs are braised in a red wine sauce with bacon, mushrooms, and herbs.

  • Chicken: Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • Seasonings: Salt and ground black pepper
  • Bacon: Adds meaty flavor and depth
  • Vegetables: Mushrooms, onions, shallots
  • Flour and butter: For thickening
  • Wine: Burgundy (or Pinot Noir)
  • Thyme: Fresh thyme
  • Broth: Chicken broth, store-bought or homemade
Crème Brûlée

Crème brûlée is a decadent French dessert consisting of a custard base and a caramelized sugar crust.

  • Egg yolks: Rich custard base
  • White sugar: 6 tablespoons (4 for custard, 2 for caramelized topping)
  • Vanilla extract: Dash for flavor
  • Cream: Heavy whipping cream
  • Brown sugar: For caramelized crust


Italian Cuisine

Italian cuisine is one of the most beloved and influential in the world, celebrated for its simplicity, freshness, and bold flavors. Rooted in ancient Roman traditions and shaped by regional diversity, Italian cooking focuses on seasonal ingredients, olive oil, fresh herbs, and artisanal cheeses. Pasta, pizza, and risotto are global favorites, but each region of Italy has its own specialties — from the hearty ragù of Bologna to the seafood-rich dishes of Sicily. Italian cuisine has inspired countless variations worldwide, but its soul remains in the balance between rustic tradition and refined artistry, where every meal is a celebration of family, culture, and passion for food.

Spaghetti alla Carbonara

Carbonara is a Roman dish made with eggs, hard cheese, and cured pork. Its signature rich and silky sauce comes from beaten eggs tossed with hot pasta.

  • Oil: Olive oil
  • Guanciale: Cured pork cheek
  • Spaghetti noodles
  • 3 large eggs (yolks)
  • Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Salt and pepper
Risotto alla Milanese

Risotto alla Milanese is a classic dish from Lombardy, Italy. Its iconic yellow color comes from saffron added during cooking.

  • 1 ¾ cups uncooked Arborio rice
  • ½ cup unsalted butter, divided
  • 1 ½ quarts beef stock
  • 3 tablespoons beef marrow
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon saffron powder
  • ¾ cup dry white wine
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 ½ cups grated Parmesan cheese
Osso Buco

Osso buco is an Italian dish of braised veal shanks, slow-cooked to melt-in-your-mouth perfection.

  • 2 pounds veal shanks
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ cup butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 large carrot, chopped
  • ⅔ cup dry white wine
  • ⅔ cup beef stock
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14.5 oz)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Japanese Cuisine

Japanese cuisine is refined, balanced, and deeply tied to nature and seasonality. It emphasizes fresh, high-quality ingredients prepared with minimal intervention to highlight their natural flavors. Staples like rice, noodles, and seafood are elevated with umami-rich elements such as miso, soy sauce, and dashi broth. Meals are often designed to balance colors, textures, and tastes—sweet, salty, sour, bitter, and savory—creating harmony on the plate.

Tonkatsu (とんかつ)

This breaded and fried pork cutlet recipe is for a time-honored Japanese dish, tonkatsu. Serve with sticky rice and tonkatsu sauce. Also great if substituted with chicken breasts.

  • 4 (4 ounce) boneless pork chops
  • Salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 cups oil for frying, or as needed
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • ¾ cup panko bread crumbs
Sukiyaki (すき焼き)

Sukiyaki features vegetables, noodles, tofu, and steak served in a steaming, flavorful broth made with dashi, mirin, and soy sauce. Traditionally cooked at the table in simmering broth; here it's cooked on stovetop.

  • 1 ½ cups prepared dashi stock
  • ¾ cup soy sauce
  • ¾ cup mirin
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 8 ounces shirataki noodles
  • 3 tablespoons canola oil, divided
  • 1 pound beef top sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 medium carrots, thinly sliced
  • 2 stalks celery, thinly sliced
  • 4 ounces fresh mushrooms, sliced (button, shiitake, or enoki)
  • 5 green onions, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 (14 ounce) package firm tofu, cubed
Yakitori (焼き鳥)

Yakitori can be literally translated to "grilled chicken," consisting of bite-sized pieces of meat (usually chicken) served on a bamboo skewer.

  • 2 pounds chicken thighs or breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Skewers (soaked in water for 30 minutes)
  • Yakitori sauce: Soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar

Chinese Cuisine

Chinese cuisine is one of the world’s oldest and most diverse culinary traditions, with flavors and techniques shaped by thousands of years of history. It emphasizes balance — not just of taste (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and umami) but also of color, texture, and aroma. Ingredients like rice, noodles, soy products, fresh vegetables, and a wide variety of meats and seafood form the foundation, while seasonings such as ginger, garlic, soy sauce, and chili create complex layers of flavor.

Peking Duck

Peking duck is a famous Chinese dish known for its crispy skin and tender meat. Traditionally made with a specific duck and prepared over several days, this version comes together in just under four hours.

  • Prepare the duck
  • Season the duck (inside and out) and stuff with green onions
  • Cover and refrigerate duck for at least 2 hours or overnight
  • Steam the duck breast-side up on a rack set in a wok or pot
  • Prick skin all over, place in roasting pan, roast 30 minutes
  • Brush honey and soy sauce over the duck
  • Increase heat and roast until brown
  • Make the plum sauce
  • Serve with plum sauce, green onions, orange slices, and parsley
Kung Pao Chicken

Kung Pao chicken (Gong Bao) is a stir-fried Chinese dish with cubed chicken in a sweet, spicy, and savory sauce. Originated in southwestern China in the early 1800s.

  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 tablespoons white wine, divided
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce, divided
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast halves, cut into chunks
  • 1 ounce hot chili paste
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
  • 1 (8 ounce) can water chestnuts
  • 4 ounces chopped peanuts
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic
Sweet and Sour Pork

Sweet and Sour Pork is a Cantonese favorite where pork is lightly battered, deep-fried, and coated in a tangy-sweet sauce. Crunchy, juicy, with a perfect balance of sweet and sour flavors.

  • 1 1/4 pound pork tenderloin, trimmed
  • Salt and ground black pepper
  • ⅓ cup ketchup
  • ⅓ cup seasoned rice vinegar
  • 1 (8 ounce) can pineapple chunks, drained (juice reserved)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 teaspoons hot chili sauce (like Sriracha)
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes

Indian Cuisine

Indian cuisine is a vibrant and diverse culinary tradition, defined by its bold flavors, aromatic spices, and regional variety. Staples like rice, lentils, wheat (for flatbreads like roti and naan), and vegetables are combined with an array of spices — cumin, coriander, turmeric, cardamom, and chili — to create rich, complex dishes.

Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken is a classic North Indian dish made with tender chicken cooked in a rich, creamy tomato-based sauce, flavored with butter, cream, and aromatic spices like garam masala, cumin, and coriander. It’s mildly spiced, slightly sweet, and perfect with naan or rice.

  • 1 cup butter, divided
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 1 (15 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 3 cups heavy cream
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 ½ pounds skinless, boneless chicken breast, cut into bite-sized chunks
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons tandoori masala
Chicken Biryani

Chicken Biryani is a fragrant and flavorful Indian rice dish cooked with basmati rice, aromatic spices, and usually meat (like chicken, lamb, or beef) or vegetables. Layers of rice and marinated meat are slow-cooked together, giving it a rich aroma and a perfect blend of spices.

  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch, dissolved in 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 tablespoons white wine, divided
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce, divided
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cut into chunks
  • 1 ounce hot chili paste
  • 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
  • 1 (8 ounce) can water chestnuts
  • 4 ounces chopped peanuts
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon chopped garlic
Paneer Tikka Masala

Paneer Tikka Masala is a popular Indian appetizer made from cubes of paneer (Indian cottage cheese) marinated in yogurt and spices, then grilled or roasted until slightly charred. It’s smoky, flavorful, and often served with mint chutney.

  • ¼ cup butter
  • 1 pound paneer, cut into 1/2-inch cubes
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 1 medium green bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 medium jalapeño peppers, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ground cashews
  • 1 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 (16 ounce) can tomato sauce

Thai Cuisine

Thai cuisine is vibrant, aromatic, and all about balancing sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors. It uses fresh herbs, chili, coconut milk, fish sauce, and ingredients like rice, noodles, seafood, and vegetables to create dishes that are bold, colorful, and full of contrast.

Pad Thai

Pad Thai is a classic Thai stir-fried noodle dish made with rice noodles, eggs, tofu or shrimp, peanuts, bean sprouts, and a tangy-sweet tamarind sauce. It’s flavorful, slightly sweet, tangy, and has that perfect balance of textures.

  • 1 (12 ounce) package rice noodles
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast halves, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • ⅛ tablespoon crushed red pepper
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • ¼ cup crushed peanuts
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
Massaman Curry

Massaman Curry is a rich, mildly spiced Thai curry with influences from Indian and Malay cuisines. It’s made with coconut milk, tender meat (like beef or chicken), potatoes, peanuts, and warm spices like cinnamon and cardamom, giving it a comforting, slightly sweet, and aromatic flavor.

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 tablespoons curry paste
  • 1 (3/4 inch thick) slice ginger, minced
  • 1 ¼ pounds skinless, boneless chicken breast meat - cubed
  • ⅓ cup peanut butter
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • 3 cups peeled, cubed potatoes
  • 1 (13.5 ounce) can coconut milk
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
Pad Krapow Gai

Pad Krapow Gai is a stir-fry dish made with finely chopped or ground chicken cooked with chile peppers, garlic, and shallots, and seasoned with a sweet and salty sauce and finished with fresh basil. It's a classic dish in Thai cuisine and can be found in restaurants and as a popular street food.

  • ⅓ cup chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce, or as needed
  • 2 teaspoons fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken thighs, coarsely chopped
  • ¼ cup sliced shallots
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons minced Thai chilies, Serrano, or other hot pepper
  • 1 cup very thinly sliced fresh basil leaves
  • 2 cups hot cooked rice

Mediterranean Cuisine

Mediterranean cuisine emphasizes fresh, wholesome ingredients like olive oil, vegetables, fruits, grains, fish, and lean meats. It’s known for its vibrant flavors, heart-healthy dishes, and simple yet elegant combinations that highlight natural ingredients.

Hummus

Hummus is a staple Mediterranean dish—creamy chickpea dip blended with tahini, olive oil, lemon, and garlic. Loved across Greece, Lebanon, and beyond, it’s perfect as a healthy appetizer, snack, or side.

  • 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas, drained
  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ cup water
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin (Optional)
  • 1 pinch paprika
  • 1 sprig fresh parsley, chopped
Falafel

Falafel is a popular Mediterranean dish of deep-fried balls made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices. Crispy on the outside and tender inside, it’s often served in pita bread with fresh vegetables, tahini, or yogurt sauce.

  • 1 cup dry garbanzo beans
  • ½ yellow onion, diced
  • ½ cup chopped fresh flat-leaf parsley
  • 4 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour, or more as needed
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • 1 ½ teaspoons salt, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground coriander
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda
  • ⅛ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • oil for frying
Shakshuka

Shakshuka is a flavorful Mediterranean and Middle Eastern dish of eggs poached in a spiced tomato and pepper sauce. Often seasoned with garlic, onions, and paprika, it’s typically served for breakfast or brunch with bread.

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • ½ cup sliced fresh mushrooms
  • 1 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 cup diced red bell pepper
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, seeded and sliced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 (28 ounce) can crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • ½ cup water, or more as needed
  • 6 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley

Middle Eastern Cuisine

Middle Eastern cuisine is rich, diverse, and rooted in ancient culinary traditions from countries like Lebanon, Israel, Jordan, and Iran. It emphasizes fresh ingredients, bold spices, and balanced flavors. Common staples include grains like rice and bulgur, legumes such as chickpeas and lentils, fresh vegetables, olive oil, and aromatic herbs. Dishes often feature grilled meats, stews, flatbreads, and flavorful dips like hummus and baba ghanoush.

Kebabs

Kebabs are skewered and grilled pieces of meat—often lamb, beef, or chicken—marinated with aromatic spices, herbs, and sometimes yogurt. They’re a staple of Middle Eastern cuisine, served with flatbreads, rice, or fresh vegetables.

  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 pound ground lamb
  • 3 tablespoons grated onion
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 tablespoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ tablespoon ground cinnamon
  • ½ teaspoon ground allspice
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 28 bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes
Baklava

Baklava is a traditional pastry known for its sweet, rich flavor and flaky texture. It consists of phyllo (or filo) dough, nuts, spices, and a sugary syrup.

  • 1 (16 ounce) package phyllo dough
  • 1 pound chopped nuts
  • 1 cup butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • ½ cup honey
Fattoush

Fattoush is a refreshing Middle Eastern salad made with fresh vegetables like tomatoes, cucumbers, radishes, and lettuce, tossed with crispy pieces of toasted or fried pita bread. Flavored with sumac, olive oil, and lemon juice, it’s tangy and zesty.

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil, for frying
  • 2 small pita breads, torn into pieces
  • 1 large English cucumber, finely diced
  • 3 cups halved grape tomatoes
  • ¾ cup chopped Italian parsley
  • ¾ cup chopped fresh mint
  • ½ red onion, finely diced
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, or to taste
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice, or to taste
  • 1 teaspoon ground sumac, or to taste
  • 1 clove garlic, crushed, or more to taste
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 ounce sheep's milk feta cheese

Korean Cuisine

Korean cuisine is all about bold, balanced flavors with an emphasis on fermentation, spice, and variety. Staples include rice, noodles, and vegetables, often accompanied by kimchi (fermented vegetables), gochujang (chili paste), and soy-based sauces. Meals usually feature multiple side dishes (banchan), highlighting harmony between sweet, sour, salty, bitter, and spicy tastes.

Kimchi

Kimchi is a traditional Korean dish made with fermented vegetables. Its ingredients can vary, but napa cabbage and Korean radish are common bases. Kimchi also often includes green onions, ginger, and garlic.

  • 2 heads napa cabbage
  • 1 ¼ cups coarse sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 5 green onions, chopped
  • ½ small white onion, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, pressed
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 5 tablespoons gochugaru (Korean chili powder)
Bibimbap

Bibimbap is a mixed rice bowl topped with sautéed and seasoned vegetables, a fried or raw egg, and usually some sliced meat (like beef). Everything is stirred together with gochujang (spicy red chili paste) just before eating, giving a perfect balance of flavors, textures, and colors.

  • 1 English cucumber, cut into matchsticks
  • ¼ cup gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste) (Optional)
  • 1 bunch fresh spinach, cut into thin strips
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil, divided
  • 2 carrots, cut into matchsticks
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 pinch red pepper flakes
  • 1 pound thinly-sliced beef top round steak
  • 4 large eggs
  • 4 cups cooked white rice
  • 4 teaspoons toasted sesame oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • 2 teaspoons gochujang, divided (Optional)
Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean street food made from chewy rice cakes (tteok) cooked in a spicy, sweet red chili sauce called gochujang. Often, it includes fish cakes, boiled eggs, and green onions.

  • 3 cups water
  • 2 dried anchovies, or more to taste
  • 3 tablespoons chile paste
  • 2 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon corn syrup
  • 2 Korean fish cakes, sliced
  • ½ onion, thickly sliced
  • 1 spring onion, thickly sliced
