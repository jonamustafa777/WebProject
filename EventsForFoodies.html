The Biggest Food Events In The World

Discover upcoming food festivals, cultural celebrations, and special events that bring people together through cuisine and tradition.

Oktoberfest (Munich, Germany)

When: Late September → first weekend in October (16–18 days)

Millions of people flock to Munich to enjoy Bavarian beer served in 1-liter steins, giant pretzels, roasted chicken, pork knuckles, sausages, and potato dumplings. There are also folk parades, music, and traditional clothing (lederhosen and dirndls).

Napoli Pizza Village (Naples, Italy)

When: Usually early June

Seafront lined with giant pizza ovens and pizzaiolos showing off their craft. Visitors can try countless pizza styles, attend concerts, pizza-making workshops, and even witness Guinness World Record attempts.

EuroChocolate (Perugia, Italy)

When: Mid–late October

One of Europe’s biggest chocolate festivals. Streets of Perugia turn into an open-air chocolate market, with sculptures, tastings, hot chocolate fountains, and chocolate-themed gadgets.

Galway International Oyster & Seafood Festival (Galway, Ireland)

When: Last weekend of September

Celebrates Ireland’s seafood heritage. Oyster-shucking competitions, seafood tastings, parades, cooking demos, and live music highlight this festival.

Alba Truffle Festival (Alba, Piedmont, Italy)

When: Every weekend in October + November.

Dedicated to the prestigious white truffle of Alba. Features a truffle market, auctions, tastings, wine pairings, and chef demonstrations. Known as a “luxury” food festival, it’s where gourmet meets rustic Italian tradition.

La Tomatina (Buñol, Spain)

When: Last Wednesday of August.

The world’s most famous food fight. Thousands of participants gather in the streets and throw overripe tomatoes at each other in a massive, joyful mess. It’s technically not about eating but has become an iconic cultural event tied to Spanish tradition.

Lucerne Cheese Festival (Lucerne, Switzerland)

When: 1st Saturday of September.

A celebration of Swiss cheese-making. More than 200 cheese varieties are showcased — from Emmental to Gruyère. Includes tastings, cheese carving, competitions, and a bustling marketplace in the old town square.

Taste of Chicago (Chicago, USA)

When: Early July.

One of the world’s largest free-admission food festivals. Chicago’s famous deep-dish pizza, hot dogs, ribs, international cuisines, and pop-up restaurants all line the city’s Grant Park. The event is family-friendly with concerts, cooking classes, and food trucks.
